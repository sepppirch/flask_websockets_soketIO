<h2 id="about">About</h2><p><strong>:3</strong>, also referred to as the “<a class="internal-link" href="/memes/subcultures/cats">cat</a> face,” is an <a class="internal-link" href="/memes/emoticons">emoticon</a> mimicking a cute facial expression often worn by <a class="internal-link" href="/memes/moe">moe</a> characters featured in <a class="internal-link" href="/memes/cultures/japan-state">Japanese</a> <a class="internal-link" href="/memes/cultures/anime-manga">manga</a> and animes. In text and <a class="internal-link" href="/memes/cultures/the-internet">online</a> communications, the emoticon is most commonly used to convey affection or a sense of childlike innocence.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div class="anyclip">
<!-- anyclip player -->
<script pubname="knowyourmemecom" src="https://play.anyclip.com/anyclip-widget/lre-widget/prod/v1/src/lre.js" widgetname="0011r000024GCSI_11030">
</script>
<!-- end anyclip player -->
</div>
<div class="entry-section-container">
<div class="entry-section">
<h2 id="origin">Origin</h2><p>:3 can be seen as a horizontal variant of (‘ω’), a well-known Japanese emoticon representing a <a class="auto-link" href="/memes/cultures/cats">cat</a>’s face that first became popular through its frequently usage on Japanese text and <a class="internal-link" href="/memes/subcultures/imageboard">image board</a> sites, most notably on <a class="internal-link" href="/memes/sites/2channel">2channel</a>, as well as other online communities since the early 2000s.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div class="entry-section-container">
<div class="entry-section">
<h2 id="spread">Spread</h2><p>On September 28th, 2003, <a class="internal-link" href="/memes/sites/urban-dictionary">Urban Dictionary</a><sup class="footnote" id="fnr1"><a class="footnote-superscript" href="#fn1">[1]</a></sup> user Alex submitted the earliest known English-language definition for :3, describing it as a <strong>Japanese</strong> emoticon for the anime “cat face.”</p><p><br/></p><center>“<a href="/photos/998722" target="_blank"><img alt=":3 A symbol meant to represent the cat face made by anime characters when they says or sarcastic, or are commenting on something cute. clever, &quot;Isn't it just adorable?:3 by Alex September 28, 2003 Cat text blue font product line paper material" class="kym-image image-auto-link" data-src="https://i.kym-cdn.com/photos/images/masonry/000/998/722/f0a.jpg" id="photo_998722" src="https://s.kym-cdn.com/assets/blank-8f52844e31c600b21b78a8b0b00dbfbb.gif" style="max-width: 425px !important;" title="Urban Dictionary - :3"/></a> ”:/photos/998722 “<a href="/photos/998721" target="_blank"><img alt="Colon Three Word version of the smiley:3 its pretty much a hamster face,sort of a but mouth You use the when your happy,blissful,peaceful,etc. Look at my new shoes!:3 Colon three! by maddiechan December 17, 2006 text blue font line product" class="kym-image image-auto-link" data-src="https://i.kym-cdn.com/photos/images/masonry/000/998/721/efd.jpg" id="photo_998721" src="https://s.kym-cdn.com/assets/blank-8f52844e31c600b21b78a8b0b00dbfbb.gif" style="max-width: 425px !important;" title="Urban Dictionary - Colon Three"/></a> ”:/photos/998721</center><br/><p>On May 30th, 2005, Urban Dictionary<sup class="footnote" id="fnr2"><a class="footnote-superscript" href="#fn2">[2]</a></sup> user Jeshua submitted an entry for <strong>=3</strong>, defining it as an emoticon expressing “cuteness or affection,” and on December 17th, 2006, Urban Dictionary user maddiechan submitted an entry for "colon three," a textual transliteration of the punctuation mark and the number that make up the emoticon, described as a "hamster face, sort of a butt-mouth" used when one feels happy or blissful. On April 17th, 2009, <a class="internal-link" href="/memes/sites/youtube">YouTuber</a> Ray William Johnson launched the web series titled “=3” in reference to the emoticon. On June 14th, 2010, an anonymous Quora<sup class="footnote" id="fnr3"><a class="footnote-superscript" href="#fn3">[3]</a></sup> user submitted the question “What does the emoticon :3 mean?”, to which user Matt Carter replied that it was a “cat face” used to “express happiness.” On May 1st, 2013, a page for “Cat Smile” was created for the manga and anime facial expression on <a class="internal-link" href="/memes/sites/tv-tropes">TV Tropes</a>,<sup class="footnote" id="fnr4"><a class="footnote-superscript" href="#fn4">[4]</a></sup> which distinguished the implication of the smile as playfully mischievous rather than manipulative or sinister.</p>
